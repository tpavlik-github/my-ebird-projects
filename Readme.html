This tool processes your personal eBird observation data and generates summaries and statistics specifically for bird sightings in Michigan, USA.

üìÅ Getting Started

Download your eBird data

  1. Log in to your eBird.org account.
  2. Go to My eBird ‚Üí Download My Data.
  3. Submit the request. You will receive an email (generally within a few minutes) with a link to download a .zip file.
  4. Upload the .zip file (or the extracted MyEBirdData.csv) using the "Choose File" button on the main app page.
    5. Tip: It doesn't matter the name of the file, just don't change the structure of the data in the csv file.

üß† What the App Does

  • Filters to only include data from US-MI (Michigan)
  • Excludes:
    • Species with slashes (/) or sp. (uncertain IDs)
    • Hybrids and domestic species
  • Handles "countable" species that have parentheses.. Ex: Dark-eyed Junco (Slate-colored)
  • On appropriate tabs, allows for species to be displayed taxonomically or alphabetically via toggle
  • On appropriate tabs, allows for sorting by clicking on the column heading

üß≠ Tabs Overview

  • Upload: Upload your CSV or ZIP file
  • County Totals: Unique species per county. Sortable by clicking on either heading.
  • Species by County: List all species seen by selected county. Can be displayed in either Taxonomical or Alphabetical order.
  • Monthly Totals: Unique species by calendar month
  • Year Totals: Unique species by year. Sortable by clicking on either heading.
  • Month Totals by Year: Species per month for each year
  • Regional Life/Ticks: Life lists & species ticks by region
  • Species by Year: List all species seen by selected year. Can be displayed in either Taxonomical or Alphabetical order.

üêõ Known Limitations

  • No mobile layout optimization (desktop recommended)
  • Only works with data from My eBird Data in the downloaded format
